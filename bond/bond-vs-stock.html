Bonds and Stocks

  • Recently, the distinct characteristics of bonds and stocks have been blurring due to the diversification of issued securities driven by issuers seeking easier capital funding and investors demanding more segmented and advanced investment products.

  • Generally speaking, stockholders can participate in decision-making processes as shareholders in shareholder meetings, while bondholders cannot participate in such processes.

  • Additionally, stock issuance involves an increase in equity capital, whereas bond issuance involves an increase in debt capital (liabilities). In the event of a company's liquidation, bondholders have priority over stockholders in receiving repayments.
