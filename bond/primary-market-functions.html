Functions of the Issuance Market ​

Functions of the Issuance Market

  • When there is a need to raise a large amount of long-term capital directly, bonds are issued to convert the funds of unspecified investors into industrial capital, thereby playing a foundational role in the development of the national economy.

Function of Open Market Operations ​

Open Market Operations

  • The function of open market operations is carried out by adjusting liquidity in the market through the buying and selling of monetary stabilization bonds (used for monetary control), such as Monetary Stabilization Securities and Foreign Exchange Stabilization Fund Bonds.
  • Open market operations are a policy tool used by central banks to adjust the money supply by increasing or decreasing liquidity in financial institutions and the private sector through the buying and selling of government bonds and other securities. At the same time, it aims to control "market interest rates" by influencing the yields of securities.
  • This policy tool is considered the most advanced among central bank policy instruments because its effects are reflected through the price mechanism of the financial market, and central banks can flexibly determine the timing and scale of operations as needed. As a result, most advanced economies currently use this as their primary monetary policy tool.
