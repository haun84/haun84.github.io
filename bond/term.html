Issuer ​

Issuer

The issuer is the entity that issues the bond and raises capital through the bond issuance.
Government bonds are issued by the Minister of Strategy and Finance based on the Government Bond Act. Other major issuers in the bond market include local governments, special corporations such as Korea Electric Power Corporation, banks, credit card companies, other financial institutions, and general corporations.

Issue Date ​

Issue Date

The issue date is the date on which the bond is issued, and it is also the day when the issuance amount is paid to the issuer. Most bonds are issued through electronic registration with the Korea Securities Depository.

Maturity Date ​

Maturity

The maturity date is the final date on which the remaining principal and interest are repaid.
Typically, bonds pay interest periodically over their term and repay the principal in a lump sum at maturity. Amortizing bonds (e.g., bonds issued with equal principal and interest repayment terms) repay part of the principal along with interest over the bond’s term until maturity.

Trading Date / Settlement Date ​

Trading Date / Settlement Date

Settlement refers to the exchange of the bond and payment of its price. The trading date is when the transaction is executed, and the settlement date is when the payment is made. The trading date and settlement date can be the same or different (for example, in the Korea Exchange stock market, settlement occurs on the trading date + 2 days).
The domestic bond market is divided into an on-exchange market (via the stock exchange) and an off-exchange or OTC market (Over The Counter). In the OTC market, it is customary to settle transactions on the next business day (T+1, or T+3 in the case of weekends), although same-day settlement is possible if agreed upon by both parties. In the Korea Exchange bond market, settlement takes place immediately on the trading date.

Face Value ​

Face Value

Face value (par value or maturity value) refers to the amount the issuer agrees to repay at maturity.
The interest paid by the issuer is determined by multiplying the face value by the coupon rate. In Korea, the standard face value for bonds is typically 10,000 KRW.
When purchasing a bond already issued in the market, the investor may buy it at a premium (e.g., 10,150 KRW per face value of 10,000 KRW) or at a discount (e.g., 9,984 KRW per face value of 10,000 KRW).

Coupon Rate ​

Coupon Rate

The coupon rate is the interest rate the issuer agrees to pay annually. It is a separate concept from bond yield.

Interest ​

Interest

Interest refers to the amount the issuer pays according to a predetermined schedule. The interest amount is calculated as 'face value × coupon rate'.
For example, if a bond has a remaining maturity of 10 years, a coupon rate of 2%, and pays interest twice a year, the investor receives 100 KRW every six months, totaling 200 KRW in interest per year.
