Structure of the Issuance Market ​

Bond Issuance Market

  • The bond issuance market refers to the entire process where bonds newly issued by issuers (such as corporations, governments, local governments, and financial institutions), which are the capital demanders, are sold to general or institutional investors, thereby transferring capital from investors to the issuers. This market is also known as the primary market.
  • The issuance market consists of the issuer, the issuing institution, and the investor. There are two methods of issuance: the direct issuance method, where the issuer directly raises funds from investors, and the indirect issuance method, where an issuing institution underwrites the bonds and sells them to investors.

Issuer ​

The issuer is the entity that raises funds by issuing bonds, and this includes the government, public institutions, corporations established by special law, and joint-stock companies under commercial law.

Investor ​

Investors are entities that respond to the offering or sale of bonds in the bond issuance market by acquiring bonds issued by the issuer. In doing so, they act as capital suppliers in the form of bond investments.

Issuing Institution ​

The issuing institution handles all tasks related to the bond issuance between the issuer and the investor, assuming risks and sales functions on behalf of the issuer.

It is common for multiple issuing institutions to jointly participate in the issuance of a single security to distribute responsibility and risk, facilitating the smooth sale of the issued securities. Issuing institutions can be categorized into lead managers, underwriters, and subscription institutions based on their roles.

Lead Managers ​

Lead Managers

  • The lead manager is responsible for overseeing all tasks related to the bond issuance, including administrative processes and providing advice related to the issuance. It also forms the underwriting syndicate.
  • If the issuance size is large, a syndicate is formed to jointly manage the process. The company playing the central role is called the lead underwriter, while the others are referred to as co-managers.

Underwriters ​

Underwriters

  • Underwriters collaborate with the lead managers and play the underwriting function, directly purchasing the issued bonds and enabling the issuer to raise substantial funds.
  • They may hold the underwritten bonds themselves or sell them to general investors and subscription institutions.

Subscription Institutions ​

Subscription Institutions

  • Unlike underwriters, who bear the risk of underwriting, subscription institutions only act as agents, handling the subscription process for unspecified investors who wish to purchase newly issued bonds.
  • The subscription process is generally carried out through the branch networks of securities companies authorized to handle underwriting.
