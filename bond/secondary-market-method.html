Bond Trading System

  • To trade bonds, investors must open an account with a securities company and can trade either through on-exchange or over-the-counter transactions.

On-Exchange Trading ​

On-Exchange Trading

  • On-exchange trading refers to transactions conducted through competitive bidding at the stock exchange.
  • Therefore, bonds available for trading are limited to listed bonds, with standardized trading hours and conditions.

Eligible Bonds

  • For a bond to be traded on-exchange, it must be listed on the stock exchange.
  • Information on whether a bond is listed can be found on the Korea Exchange (KRX) website or by inquiring with the bond listing team of the stock exchange.

Quotes and Price Limits

  • A quote is the act of expressing the intention to buy or sell a bond.
  • While it is common to quote bond yields, in the exchange market, bond quotes are based on price.
  • There are two types of price quotes: limit order, where the investor specifies a price, and market order, where the trade is executed at the market price.
  • For regular bonds, only limit orders are used, but market orders are allowed for small bonds.
  • Unlike the stock market, the bond market does not have price limits.

Trading Units

  • The trading unit is the minimum face value amount that can be traded on the exchange, and trades must be in multiples of this unit.
  • Currently, on the exchange, bond prices are based on a face value of 10,000 KRW, and trades are executed in units of 100,000 KRW face value.
  • For stock-related bonds, trades are possible in units of 100,000 KRW face value, while small government and public bonds can be traded in units of 1,000 KRW.
  • However, as of now, small government bonds are generally traded in units of 10,000 KRW or higher.

Trading Hours

  • Trading hours are set from Monday to Friday, from 9:00 AM to 3:30 PM.

Trade Execution and Settlement

  • Trades are automatically executed through an electronic trading system.
  • Initially, trades are conducted using a single-price competitive bidding system during simultaneous bids, followed by continuous trading through multiple-price competitive bids.
  • Settlement for the transaction is completed on the same day through cash and securities transfer.

Commission Fees

  • Investors who trade bonds must pay a commission to the securities company. Current commission rates are determined by individual securities companies.
  • Generally, the commission is adjusted based on the remaining period from the trading day to the bond's maturity.

Over-The-Counter (OTC) Trading ​

OTC Trading

  • OTC trading refers to transactions conducted outside of the exchange, either through proprietary trading or brokerage.

Eligible Bonds

  • The OTC market covers both listed and unlisted bonds.
  • However, certain bonds issued within two months of the trading day, such as Type 1 National Housing Bonds, Seoul Subway Bonds, and Regional Development Bonds, must be traded on the exchange if the face value is less than 50 million KRW, in accordance with the relevant regulations.

Quotes and Price Limits

  • Quotes are generally given in terms of yield. After quoting based on yield, the price is calculated by adding or subtracting based on the yield per 10,000 KRW face value, with no price limits.

Trading Units

  • In the OTC market, trading units are typically in multiples of 10 billion KRW face value.

Trading Hours

  • There are no specific restrictions on trading hours, but transactions usually occur during the main business hours of financial institutions, from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM.

Trade Execution and Settlement

  • Settlement is completed the next day, with cash and securities exchanged the day after the transaction is executed.

Commission Fees

  • There are no commission fees for OTC trades, as securities companies earn a spread from the difference between the buy and sell prices.
  • However, a commission fee may be charged for brokerage trades.
