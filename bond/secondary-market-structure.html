Structure of the Secondary Market

  • The secondary market can be divided into the organized market, known as the Exchange Market (on-exchange market), and the unorganized market, known as the OTC Market (Over The Counter Market), based on the form of market organization.
    • The Exchange Market is an organized and continuous market where listed bonds are traded through competitive trading, with a large number of buy and sell orders concentrated in one place.
  • On the other hand, the OTC Market is an unorganized market where both listed and unlisted bonds are traded individually through negotiated transactions between customers and securities companies, between securities companies, or between customers.

Exchange Market ​

Exchange Market

  • Bond trading in the exchange market takes place collectively at a designated location called a stock exchange.
  • Bonds that are traded must be listed on the exchange.
  • Additionally, the trading conditions are standardized, and the trading hours are limited.

OTC Market (Over The Counter Market) ​

OTC Market

  • While the stock secondary market focuses primarily on exchange-based brokerage trades, where intermediaries process orders from investors,
  • In the case of bonds in Korea, the secondary market operates primarily through over-the-counter (OTC) trading.
  • Unlike stocks, bonds are difficult for individual investors to handle, so they are usually traded in large volumes between institutional investors such as financial institutions and corporations.
  • Furthermore, bonds are traded more through negotiated deals rather than competitive individual trades, and since bonds must be listed for on-exchange trading, OTC transactions make up the majority of bond trades.
  • In many foreign countries, bond listing on exchanges is not mandatory. Even for listed bonds, trading through the exchange is typically only required for certain transactions to protect small investors.
